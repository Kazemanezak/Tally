# Unit 7 Brainstorming — Tally

## Step 1: Brainstorming Ideas

Below are the initial app ideas our team considered before choosing a final project.

1. **Tally** — A habit tracker that helps users build good habits and break bad ones using streaks, milestone celebrations, and satisfying animations.
2. **PantryPal** — A kitchen inventory app that tracks food at home, expiration dates, and suggests meals based on what the user already has.
3. **StudySprint** — A focused study timer app with session streaks, quick goal-setting, and visual progress toward assignments or exams.
4. **FitBuddy** — A lightweight workout tracker for logging exercises, routines, and weekly fitness goals.
5. **MoodMap** — A daily mood journaling app that lets users check in emotionally and look for patterns over time.
6. **BudgetBite** — A simple spending tracker for students and young adults that categorizes purchases and shows where money is going.
7. **WaterWise** — A hydration reminder app that helps users meet daily water goals with quick logging and streaks.
8. **CleanSweep** — A chore and cleaning tracker that breaks household tasks into small recurring habits.

---

## Step 2: Evaluation of Top 3 Ideas

We selected the 3 strongest ideas and evaluated them using the required attributes: **Mobile, Story, Market, Habit, and Scope**.

### Idea 1 — Tally

**Concept:**
An iOS habit tracker that makes daily habit logging feel rewarding through streaks, milestone celebrations, and animated feedback.

| Attribute | Evaluation |
|-----------|------------|
| **Mobile** | Strong mobile fit. Logging a habit is a quick action that works naturally on a phone and benefits from portability, notifications, haptics, and one-handed use. |
| **Story** | Strong story. The app tells a clear story of self-improvement: small daily actions build momentum, and streaks turn progress into something emotional and visible. |
| **Market** | Broad audience. Many people want to improve routines such as exercise, reading, hydration, or avoiding unhealthy habits. |
| **Habit** | High habit potential. Users are encouraged to return daily for check-ins, streak maintenance, and milestone rewards. |
| **Scope** | Good MVP scope. The first version can stay focused on core actions: create habit, log progress, track streaks, and celebrate milestones. |

**Why it stands out:**
Tally is simple enough for an MVP, but still has a fun identity and clear value for users.

---

### Idea 2 — PantryPal

**Concept:**
A kitchen inventory app that helps users track groceries, monitor expiration dates, and reduce food waste.

| Attribute | Evaluation |
|-----------|------------|
| **Mobile** | Strong mobile use case. Users are likely to check inventory while shopping or cooking. |
| **Story** | Moderate story. The app solves a practical problem, but the emotional arc is weaker than a streak-based product. |
| **Market** | Good market. Families, students, and meal preppers could benefit from keeping track of food at home. |
| **Habit** | Moderate habit potential. Users may not engage every day unless they are highly organized or cooking frequently. |
| **Scope** | Medium difficulty. Inventory management, item editing, categorization, and expiration handling could grow complex quickly. |

**Why it was considered:**
It solves a real-world problem and has clear usefulness, but it may require more setup from users and less frequent engagement.

---

### Idea 3 — StudySprint

**Concept:**
A study productivity app where users start focused sessions, build study streaks, and track progress toward academic goals.

| Attribute | Evaluation |
|-----------|------------|
| **Mobile** | Good mobile fit. Students often study with their phones nearby, and quick timer-based interactions work well on mobile. |
| **Story** | Good story. The app supports progress and discipline, especially around exams, assignments, and productivity. |
| **Market** | Solid market. Students are a clear target audience, especially in college environments. |
| **Habit** | High habit potential during school periods, but less consistent outside of active study cycles. |
| **Scope** | Reasonable scope, though features like timers, task grouping, statistics, and session history could expand the project quickly. |

**Why it was considered:**
It has a clear audience and use case, but its appeal is narrower than a general habit tracker.

---

## Final App Idea Chosen

### **Tally — iOS Habit Tracker**

We chose **Tally** as our final app idea.

### Why we chose Tally

Tally best satisfies the required evaluation criteria:

- **Mobile:** It is designed around short, fast phone interactions.
- **Story:** It has a strong emotional feedback loop because streaks and milestones make progress feel meaningful.
- **Market:** It appeals to a broad group of users who want to improve routines or quit bad habits.
- **Habit:** It naturally encourages daily engagement.
- **Scope:** It is focused enough for an MVP while still feeling polished and unique.

### Final Description

Tally is an iOS habit tracker designed to make logging habits feel entertaining and addictive. Users can track both positive habits they want to build and negative habits they want to break. The app uses streaks, milestone celebrations, and satisfying animations to keep users motivated and engaged.

---

## Team Conclusion

After brainstorming and evaluating multiple ideas, our team selected **Tally** because it offers the best combination of user value, daily engagement, and achievable MVP scope for this capstone project.
